Cremation vs Burial: Understanding Your Options

Cremation
Benefits:
  • Generally more cost-effective
  • More flexible timing for services
  • Various options for final disposition
  • Environmentally conscious option
  • Portable - can move with family
Options for Ashes:
  • Burial in cemetery plot
  • Placement in columbarium
  • Scattering in meaningful location
  • Keeping at home in urn
  • Memorial jewelry or keepsakes
Burial
Benefits:
  • Traditional and familiar process
  • Permanent memorial location
  • Follows religious traditions
  • Provides place for visitation
  • Family plot options
Burial Options:
  • Traditional ground burial
  • Mausoleum entombment
  • Green/natural burial
  • Veteran's cemetery
  • Family cemetery plot
infoMaking the Decision

The choice between cremation and burial is deeply personal and can be influenced by religious beliefs, family traditions, environmental concerns, and practical considerations. Our compassionate team can help you understand all options and make the choice that feels right for your family.

Legal Requirements and Documentation

Required Documents
  • Death Certificate
    Official document certifying the death
  • Burial/Cremation Permit
    Legal authorization for disposition
  • Medical Certificate
    Cause of death certification by doctor
  • Identification
    Valid ID of the deceased
Timeline Requirements
  • Death must be registered within 3 working days
  • Burial/cremation permits required before disposition
  • Coroner clearance may be needed in some cases
Who Can Make Arrangements

In New Zealand, the following people can make funeral arrangements (in order of priority):

  1. Spouse or partner
  2. Adult children
  3. Parents
  4. Adult siblings
  5. Other relatives
  6. Appointed representative
Important Considerations
  • Organ Donation
    Must be arranged immediately if desired
  • Autopsy
    May be required by coroner in certain cases
  • Repatriation
    Special requirements for international transport
